Auto Driving Car Simulation


You're working on a brand new auto driving car to compete against Tesla. You've already gotten the prototype car working but rather primitively.


You're testing your prototype on a large rectangular field, with coordinates denoting the position on that field. Based on the coordinates, the bottom left position is denoted (0, 0), and the top right position is denoted (width, height).


At this moment, it can only follow these commands:

- L: rotates the car by 90 degrees to the left

- R: rotates the car by 90 degrees to the right

- F: moves forward by 1 grid point


At the same time, the car has a facing direction, which follows the usual map convention. So for a car at position (1,2) facing North and moves forward by 1 grid point, it'll end up at (1, 3), still facing North.


Sample Input

Your sample input consists of 3 lines. The first line indicates the width and height of the field. The second line indicates the current position and facing direction of the car. The last line shows the subsequent commands it will execute. For example:


10 10 1 2 N 
FFRFFFRRLF

To interpret the input above: the field is 10 by 10 in size; hence, the upper right coordinate is (9, 9). The car is at position (1, 2) facing North and executes these steps `FFRFFFRRLF` sequentially. So where would the car end up? What direction will it be facing?


If the car tries to go out of the boundary, that command is ignored. For example, for a car at (0, 0) facing South, when it receives an F command to move forward, the command is ignored, as otherwise, the car will move outside of the boundary.


Sample Output

Based on the sample input above, the output would be:

4 3 S
